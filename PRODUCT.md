# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

IT procurement managers, office administrators, and operations leads at small-to-mid-size **business offices** (not consumers). Their context: they're at a desk, under time pressure, evaluating whether to trust an outside vendor with two things that matter — keeping their Windows fleet running and sourcing/divesting Windows licenses without legal or budget risk.

The job to be done:
1. **Keep Windows working** without staffing a full internal helpdesk — a predictable, subscription-based way to resolve the recurring Windows issues that eat office productivity.
2. **Buy or sell Windows licenses** for the business through a counterparty that is transparent, legitimate, and compliant.

They arrive skeptical (the tech-support/license-resale space is full of scams) and leave only if the site reads as a credible, accountable business. Contact is **email-only by design** — a deliberate trust and paper-trail signal for B2B procurement, not a limitation.

## Product Purpose

Comsol (comsol.digital) is the marketing and lead-generation surface for an **independent B2B IT service provider** offering two lines:

- **Windows Fix subscription** — a tiered support subscription. A free, public **common-issue knowledge base** on the page solves the everyday problems (update stuck, printer offline, slow boot, activation errors) and demonstrates competence; the deeper, hands-on, SLA-backed remediation lives behind the subscription.
- **Windows license trading** — buying and selling genuine Windows licenses (volume/transferable) for business offices, with authenticity and compliance front and centre.

Success = a qualified business sends an **email enquiry** (subscription start or a license buy/sell quote) via the on-page form, which composes a pre-filled message to Comsol's inbox. There is no phone number anywhere on the site, no live checkout, and no account system — this is a credibility-first lead funnel.

The site must satisfy **Google Ads** policy for this category: honest representation, a clear and accurate business identity, no impersonation of or implied affiliation with Microsoft, no fear-based "your PC is infected" tactics, visible Privacy and Terms, and a working contact method.

## Brand Personality

**Engineered. Exact. Dependable.** The voice of a precision instrument, not a salesperson. Calm, technically literate, plain-spoken. It states what it does, how, and what it costs in clear terms; it never hypes, never scares, never over-promises. Confidence comes from specificity (named issues, real SLAs, honest disclaimers), not adjectives. Think marine navigation console / lab measurement equipment: serious, legible, accountable.

Emotional goal: a skeptical procurement reader feels **reassured that this is a real, competent, accountable business** — and slightly impressed by the craft, which itself signals competence.

## Anti-references

- **Scam tech-support sites.** Red "WARNING / VIRUS DETECTED" banners, blinking phone numbers, fake Microsoft logos and "Certified Partner" badges, countdown timers, fake live-chat agents. The entire design must read as the opposite of this.
- **Implied Microsoft affiliation.** No Microsoft/Windows logos used as if endorsed; no "official" framing. Windows is referenced only as the platform we service, with an explicit non-affiliation disclaimer.
- **Generic SaaS slop.** Purple-blue gradient hero, three identical icon-cards, a hero-metric template, gradient text, an uppercase tracked eyebrow above every section.
- **Editorial-magazine cosplay.** This is not a Fraunces-italic broadsheet; the precision comes from engineering, not from a serif drop cap.
- **Consumer-cute.** No mascots, no playful blobs, no rounded-everything friendliness. This is for businesses.

## Design Principles

1. **Show competence, don't claim it.** The free knowledge base proving we can fix real Windows issues does more than any "trusted by thousands" badge. Demonstrate, then offer the subscription.
2. **Legitimacy is the product.** Every compliance element (non-affiliation disclaimer, Privacy/Terms, honest pricing, genuine-license language, email paper trail) is a feature surfaced with confidence, not buried fine print.
3. **Precision as aesthetic.** Exact alignment, real data in monospace, instrument-grade detailing. The craft of the page is itself the trust argument.
4. **Email is the deliberate channel.** No phone, by design — frame it as cleaner, accountable, and on-the-record for business procurement, never as a missing feature.
5. **Fast and findable.** SEO and Core Web Vitals are first-class: a credibility funnel that nobody finds, or that loads slowly, has failed. Static, semantic, structured-data-rich.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**. Body text ≥4.5:1 against its (dark) background; large text ≥3:1; placeholders meet 4.5:1. Full keyboard operability (nav, accordion KB, form) with visible focus states. Honour `prefers-reduced-motion` with non-motion alternatives for the instrument animation and scroll reveals. Don't encode meaning in color alone (the brass accent is reinforced by weight/label). Semantic landmarks and heading order for screen readers; the dark instrument theme is held to the same contrast bar as a light one.
